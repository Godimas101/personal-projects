# Quick Seafood Congee (Serves 4–6)

📊 Protein: 38g | Fat: 14g | Carbs: 45g | Calories: 480 | Protein Rating: 8/10
*(Per serving, assuming batch divided into 2 main portions + leftovers; midpoint values used)*

> **Macro notes:** Fish mix (~6–8 oz cooked per serving) ≈ 30–40g protein, 8–15g fat, 0g carbs, ~250–350 cal. ½ cup cooked basmati in congee ≈ 4g protein, 0g fat, 40–50g carbs, ~200 cal. Seasonings/oil negligible. High protein from fish, low added fat, nutrient-dense.

## Ingredients

**Base**
- 1 cup basmati rice, rinsed well
- 10–12 cups water or low-sodium chicken broth (broth gives better flavour)
- 4–6 thin slices fresh ginger
- Pinch of salt

**Proteins** (~8–12 oz total)
- Tilapia, sliced thin
- Salmon, cubed or sliced
- Fish balls, halved

**Seasonings**
- Soy sauce, to taste
- Sesame oil (if you have it)
- White pepper
- Salt
- Pinch of turmeric (optional)

**Optional veggies (elevate it big time if you grab them)**
- Thinly sliced mushrooms or shiitake
- Bok choy leaves
- Chopped green onions and/or cilantro (garnish)

## Method

**Stove:**
1. Combine rinsed rice, water/broth, ginger slices, and a pinch of salt in a large pot. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a low simmer (partly covered). Cook 1.5–2 hours, stirring occasionally — add water if it gets too thick.

**Instant Pot:**
1. Combine rinsed rice, 10–12 cups water/broth, ginger, and salt. Cook on high pressure 25–30 min + natural release 15 min (or use the manual porridge setting if available).

**Both methods:**
2. **Finish the base:** Stir vigorously to break up the grains for a creamier texture.
3. **Prep proteins:** Slice tilapia thin and marinate briefly with 1 tsp soy sauce + a pinch of cornstarch + a little ginger. Cube salmon. Halve fish balls.
4. **Add proteins:** Once congee is creamy and thick, add fish pieces and fish balls. Simmer 5–8 minutes until fish is just cooked — opaque and flakes easily. Don't overcook.
5. **Veggies and finish:** Stir in mushrooms first (2–3 min), then any quick greens like bok choy leaves (last 2 min). Season with soy sauce, a drizzle of sesame oil, white pepper, and salt to taste.
6. **Serve hot** garnished with chopped green onions and/or cilantro.

> **Tip:** Congee is usually standalone but serve with a side of rice if you want more substance. Ask for Instant Pot tweaks or a spicier version if you want to mix it up.
