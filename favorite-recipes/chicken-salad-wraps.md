# Chicken Salad Wraps (1 Can Kirkland Seasoned Chunk Chicken Breast)

📊 Protein: 62g | Fat: 12g | Carbs: 14g | Calories: 380 | Protein Rating: 10/10
*(Full recipe yield for 3–4 wraps; midpoint estimates used)*

High-protein chicken salad using canned Kirkland seasoned chicken as the base — same light style as the tuna and egg salads, but even leaner. Yogurt replaces mayo to keep it anti-inflammatory and lower fat without losing creaminess.

> **Macro notes:** 1 can Kirkland seasoned chunk chicken breast (drained, ~12.5 oz / 354 g) ≈ 55–60g protein, 6–10g fat, 0–4g carbs, ~260–300 cal. ¼ cup plain yogurt ≈ 4–6g protein, 0–2g fat, 4–6g carbs, 30–50 cal. Dijon + red onion + garlic + herbs ≈ 4–6g carbs total. Conservative totals prioritize a high protein-efficiency result.

## Ingredients

- 1 can Kirkland seasoned chunk chicken breast (drained well)
- ¼ cup plain yogurt (or kefir for a tangier result — Greek-style for extra thickness and protein)
- 1–1½ tsp Dijon mustard (or yellow mustard for milder tang)
- ¼ small red onion, finely diced
- 1 small garlic clove, minced (or ¼–½ tsp granulated garlic)
- ½ tsp dried dill weed (sub parsley or oregano if preferred)
- ½ tsp smoked paprika
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste (go light — the chicken is already seasoned)
- **For wraps:** your wraps + optional extras like sliced tomatoes, chopped olives, or bean sprouts

### Optional Boosts

| Addition | Effect |
|---|---|
| Pinch ground turmeric | Anti-inflammatory |
| Pinch ground cumin or curry powder | Warm, earthy note |
| Splash soy sauce | Umami depth |
| Pinch cayenne or crushed red pepper | Mild heat |

## Instructions

1. **Drain** the chicken thoroughly (press out liquid) and flake/shred it in a medium bowl with a fork.
2. **Make the dressing:** Mix yogurt, mustard, minced garlic, dill weed, smoked paprika, salt, pepper, and any optional boosts. Stir until smooth and creamy.
3. **Combine:** Add flaked chicken and diced red onion. Fold gently, then mash lightly for a creamy-chunky texture — not too mushy, just right for wraps.
4. **Taste and adjust:** More mustard for tang, more yogurt for creaminess, pinch of heat or salt as needed. Let sit 5–10 minutes in the fridge for flavors to meld (optional but recommended).
5. **Assemble:** Divide evenly among wraps (~⅓–½ cup per wrap). Add any extras like tomato slices or olives inside. Fold sides first to seal, roll tightly, and slice in half if desired.

Serve immediately or wrap tightly and chill briefly.
