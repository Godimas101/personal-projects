# Spiced Chicken Bone Broth Soup with Rice, Chickpeas, Red Lentils & Egg White Powder

📊 Protein: 41g | Fat: 7g | Carbs: 99g | Calories: 590 | Protein Rating: 7/10
*(Per serving; recipe yields ~6 hearty servings)*

> **Macro notes:** Original base (chicken bones + 1 cup each rice/chickpeas/red lentils) gives ~32g protein per serving. Adding ½ cup egg white powder to the whole pot boosts it by ~9g per serving (egg white powder is ~80–85% pure protein, almost zero fat/carbs). Conservative totals keep it high-protein and aligned with your 15% body fat goal while staying anti-inflammatory and nourishing.

Warm, spiced, one-pot pressure cooker soup. The rice and lentils break down into a creamy porridge-like texture. Egg white powder blends in seamlessly for a protein boost without changing the flavour.

## Ingredients (full pot, ~6 servings)

- 4 qt water + 2 lb chicken bones (pressure cooker)
- 1 cup dried jasmine rice (rinsed well)
- 1 cup dried chickpeas (rinsed)
- 1 cup dried red lentils (rinsed)
- ½ cup egg white powder (protein booster)
- 1–2 Tbsp coconut oil or olive oil (optional, for sautéing aromatics)
- 1 small red onion, diced
- 3–4 garlic cloves, minced (or 1–1½ tsp granulated garlic)
- 2–3 tsp curry powder
- 1–2 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp ground turmeric
- ½–1 tsp garam masala
- ½ tsp smoked paprika
- ½ tsp salt (or to taste)
- Freshly ground black pepper (to taste)
- Optional heat: pinch ground cayenne pepper or crushed red pepper
- Optional aromatics: 1–2 star anise or a few cloves

## Method

1. **Start the base:** Pressure cooker already has the bones + 4 qt water. Add rinsed rice, chickpeas, and red lentils directly to the pot.
2. **Sauté aromatics (optional but recommended):** On sauté mode, heat 1 Tbsp coconut oil, add diced red onion and garlic, cook 2–3 minutes until fragrant.
3. **Add spices:** Stir in curry powder, cumin, turmeric, garam masala, smoked paprika, salt, pepper, cayenne (if using), and star anise/cloves.
4. **Pressure cook:** Seal and cook on high pressure for 25–30 minutes (natural release 10–15 minutes). The rice and lentils will break down into a creamy porridge-like soup.
5. **Boost the protein:** Open the pot, remove bones (discard or pick off any meat). Stir the soup well. Sprinkle in the ½ cup egg white powder while stirring constantly — it will dissolve and thicken the soup slightly without clumps. Simmer on sauté mode for 2–3 minutes to fully incorporate and heat through. Taste and adjust seasoning (add a splash of water if it gets too thick).
6. **Finish:** Optional — stir in a spoonful of yogurt or kefir at the end for extra creaminess and probiotics.

## Serve

Hot in bowls. Leftovers thicken overnight and reheat beautifully — just add a splash of water.
